\[ v_1 = |0, 2 \rangle, v_2 = |1, 1 \rangle, v_3 = |2, 0 \rangle \]

    \section{Schmidt Decomposition}\label{schmidt-decomposition}

The schmidt decomposition is related to the SVD decomposition (for a
finite dimensional Hilbert space) as follows
\[ |\psi \rangle = \sum_i \sum_j C_{ij} |a \rangle_i \otimes |b \rangle_j = \sum_i \sqrt{p_i} | \alpha \rangle \otimes | \beta \rangle _i \]
where the SVD of the matrix \(C_{ij}\) gives vectors \(U\), \(\Sigma\),
\(V\) such that \[ C = U \Sigma V^\dagger \] and
\[ | \alpha \rangle_i = U | a \rangle_i \]
\[ | \beta \rangle_i = V^* | b \rangle_i \]
\[ \Sigma_{ii} = \sqrt{p_i} \]
